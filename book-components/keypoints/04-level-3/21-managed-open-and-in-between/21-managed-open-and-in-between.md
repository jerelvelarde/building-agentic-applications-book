# Chapter 21 — Managed, Open, and In Between

## Hook

Two organizational agents look similar in Slack. One team owns adapters, state, identity, runtime, sandboxes, memory, and on-call; the other buys a managed product that owns some—but not all—of those boundaries.

## Reader outcome

Compare direct Channels, OpenTag, CopilotKit's managed direction, and Claude Tag by responsibility, authority model, data handling, and availability.

## Core claims

- Managed vs self-hosted is a responsibility allocation, not a quality ranking.
- Compare surfaces, hosting, platform credentials, runtime, state, identity, UI, approvals, memory, audit, residency/retention, delegation, and operations.
- OpenTag main and its managed-development snapshot are different pins with different package/availability truth.
- CopilotKit Intelligence remains early access/waitlisted/internal until current official verification.
- Claude Tag is a managed reference with agent identity, ephemeral execution, policies/logs/spend/memory; its current availability and Teams status must be refreshed.

## Exercise

Write a build-versus-buy decision record. For each control, name the vendor responsibility, your responsibility, evidence, contract/API dependency, data location, exit path, and unresolved risk.

## Failure and security section

Cover outdated availability claims, self-hosting mistaken for control, managed service mistaken for transferred accountability, agent identity broader than requester, and missing export/decommission path.

## Production checklist

- [ ] Availability and channels reverified.
- [ ] Responsibility boundary documented.
- [ ] Identity/credential model explicit.
- [ ] Data/retention/residency reviewed.
- [ ] Approval/memory/audit guarantees verified.

## Quotable line target
